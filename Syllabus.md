## Description
Provides basic training in causal inference fundamentals and techniques. Topics include a refresher on linear regression and different study designs; randomized treatment assignment and A/B testing framework; the Potential outcomes model; examples of observational studies throughout history; understanding the impacts of confounding variables, covariate balancing, propensity score, and matching methods. R exercises on matching, propensity score estimation, and regression adjustment. Discussions of tools to strengthen causal inference using multiple control groups and other methods. Causal inference under repeated treatments over time: Cox model, g-computation. Difference-in-differences and synthetic control methods for policy evaluation.

## Learning Outcomes
Upon completing this course, the students will be able to:
- Develop an understanding of foundational concepts in causal inference, including study design, confounding, and causal estimands, with emphasis on their role in empirical research.
- Gain familiarity with key frameworks such as the potential outcomes model, randomization-based inference, and assumptions underlying causal models.
- Learn to critically assess observational studies and strengthen causal claims using tools like matching, propensity scores, and sensitivity analysis.
- Acquire hands-on experience in implementing causal inference techniques in R, including matching algorithms, propensity score estimation, and regression-based adjustments.
- Get introduced to causal inference tools such as instrumental variables, g-computation, Difference-in-Differences, and Synthetic Control.

## Weekly Course Topics
*This is the tentative layout of our weekly course topics.*

- **Week 1:** Refresher on linear regression and simple covariate adjustment (conceptual examples from social and behavioral sciences) + R exercises to assess implications of flawed covariate adjustment.
- **Week 2:** Randomized experimentation and A/B testing (workout examples in mobile App development).
- **Weeks 3–4:** Potential Outcomes Framework, definition of causal estimands, randomization-based inference (workout examples from agricultural and educational sciences) + R exercises for randomization inference.
- **Week 5:** Historical perspective on inference from non-randomized (observational) studies (conceptual examples from across the fields of empirical sciences). Matching as a tool for design of an observational study (workout examples from digital marketing and health sciences) + R exercises for matching.
- **Week 6-7:** Ignorability of treatment assignment, and propensity score; covariate adjustment methods.
- **Week 8 (Exam Week):** Review and Q&A and Within term Exam 1.
- **Weeks 9-10:** Impact of unmeasured confounding; strengthening causal inference under unmeasured confounding (using multiple control groups, sensitivity analysis) + R exercises.
- **Week 11:** Instrumental variables and natural experiments (workout examples in social sciences, economics).
- **Week 12:** Repeated treatments over time: Cox proportional hazards model, G-computation, and longitudinal causal inference (conceptual examples in health sciences).
- **Week 13:** Methods for policy evaluation: Difference-in-Differences, synthetic control methods + R exercises.
- **Week 14 (Exam Week):** Review and Q&A and Within term Exam 2.
